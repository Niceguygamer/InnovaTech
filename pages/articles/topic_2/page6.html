Topic # 6

Wait, What Can I Actually Be?

OBILLO, MON ANDREI March 20, 2026 5 min read

In relation to my previous blog, I am here to give you a bit of a glimpse into some specializations I have found during my own research into finding the right one for me, in hopes that it can help you navigate your own path. I decided not to add the salary grade to prevent you from choosing something based solely on income, as I've said before, you need to actually love what you do.

Software Engineering

Software Engineering focuses on designing, coding, testing, and maintaining software, often involving collaboration with other developers.

Pros: Very high demand in the job market. Versatile skill set applicable to multiple industries. Clear career progression: junior → senior → lead.

Cons: Tight deadlines can cause stress. Continuous learning required due to evolving frameworks and technologies. Some projects can become repetitive.

Workload: Moderate to heavy. Full-time roles generally expect 40–50+ hours per week; coursework may require 15–20+ hours per week outside class.

Web Development

Web Development focuses on creating and maintaining websites and web applications, involving front-end, back-end, or full-stack development.

Pros: High demand, especially for front-end, back-end, and full-stack developers. Immediate visual results for your work, which can be satisfying. Lots of resources and tutorials available for self-learning.

Cons: Rapidly evolving frameworks and libraries require constant updating. Can be repetitive if working on maintenance-heavy projects. High competition in entry-level roles.

Workload: Moderate to heavy. Expect 40+ hours per week in full-time roles; projects and assignments can take 10–20+ hours weekly for students.

Data Science

Data Science focuses on analyzing large datasets to extract insights and build predictive models using statistics, machine learning, and data visualization.

Pros: High demand across tech, finance, healthcare, and more. Strong potential for problem-solving and impactful work. Well-compensated roles in the industry.

Cons: Requires strong foundation in math, statistics, and programming. Projects can be complex and time-consuming. Constantly evolving tools and techniques.

Workload: Moderate to heavy. Full-time roles: 40–50+ hours per week. Coursework can require significant time for coding, analyzing datasets, and learning new algorithms.

Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning

AI/ML focuses on building algorithms and models that allow machines to "learn" and make predictions or decisions.

Pros: Cutting-edge field with high demand and lucrative salaries. Opportunities for research and innovation. Skills applicable to multiple industries.

Cons: Steep learning curve in mathematics, statistics, and programming. Projects often require large computational resources. Continuous learning is mandatory due to rapid field evolution.

Workload: Heavy. Expect 40–50+ hours per week in full-time roles, plus additional time for research and model training. Student projects often require significant independent study.

Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity focuses on protecting systems, networks, and data from cyber threats and vulnerabilities.

Pros: Extremely high demand due to growing cyber threats. Opportunities to specialize (e.g., ethical hacking, penetration testing, security analysis). High salaries and job security.

Cons: High responsibility; mistakes can have serious consequences. Constant need to stay updated with evolving threats. Stressful during security incidents or breaches.

Workload: Moderate to heavy. 40+ hours per week, often with on-call duties. Student learning may involve labs, simulations, and certifications.

Mobile Development

Mobile Development focuses on building applications for mobile devices, including Android and iOS, using native or cross-platform frameworks.

Pros: High demand for mobile apps across industries. Immediate user feedback and visible results. Opportunities to specialize in Android, iOS, or cross-platform frameworks.

Cons: Rapidly changing platforms and OS updates. App stores have strict guidelines that can delay releases. Can be repetitive if maintaining legacy apps.

Workload: Moderate to heavy. Full-time roles: 40–50+ hours per week. Student projects may require 10–20+ hours weekly for learning frameworks and deploying apps.

Cloud Computing

Cloud Computing focuses on managing, deploying, and scaling applications and services in the cloud, using platforms like AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud.

Pros: High demand in virtually every industry adopting cloud services. Opportunities to specialize in DevOps, cloud architecture, or cloud security. Skills transferable across multiple platforms and environments.

Cons: Steep learning curve for cloud architectures and services. Requires continuous learning due to platform updates. Mistakes can affect production systems significantly.

Workload: Moderate to heavy. Full-time: 40–50+ hours per week, sometimes including on-call responsibilities. Students may spend significant time setting up cloud environments and learning deployment pipelines.

DevOps

DevOps focuses on bridging development and operations to automate and improve software delivery, continuous integration, and deployment.

Pros: High demand in modern software teams. Opportunities to work across development, testing, and operations. Encourages automation and efficiency.

Cons: Can be stressful due to tight deadlines and production issues. Requires knowledge of multiple tools and scripting languages. Continuous learning is mandatory as technologies evolve.

Workload: Moderate to heavy. 40–50+ hours per week in full-time roles, with occasional on-call duties. Students may spend extra time learning CI/CD pipelines and automation tools.

Game Development

Game Development focuses on designing, programming, and testing interactive games for PC, consoles, or mobile, often involving graphics, physics, and storytelling.

Pros: Highly creative and fun for those passionate about gaming. Opportunities in indie studios or major game companies. Combines programming with art, design, and storytelling.

Cons: Highly competitive industry. Long hours, especially near project deadlines ("crunch time"). Can involve repetitive debugging and optimization tasks.

Workload: Moderate to very heavy. Full-time roles can exceed 50+ hours per week during crunch periods. Student projects can require 10"30+ hours per week depending on the complexity of the game.

IT Support / Systems Administration

IT Support and System Administration focus on maintaining, troubleshooting, and optimizing computer systems and networks within an organization.

Pros: Entry point to many IT careers. Steady demand in organizations of all sizes. Provides a strong foundation in systems, networks, and user support.

Cons: Can involve repetitive troubleshooting tasks. May require being on-call for emergencies. Less creative compared to development roles.

Workload: Moderate. Full-time: typically 40 hours per week, sometimes with after-hours support. Students may spend extra hours setting up labs or practicing network configurations.

Closing Thoughts

In choosing, you must always consider testing yourself in these specializations, as recommended in previous blogs. You can always seek for more help and guidance with those in the tech industry, or research more about it online. And finally, despite me saying that choosing a specialization is such a big deal, your first choice might not always be your last. In the end, you can always shift and change your mind, and what matters most is that you're happy with what you do.

Advice

  1. Research and understand the different specializations available.
  2. Consider testing yourself in different specializations before choosing.
  3. Don't choose based solely on salary; passion matters more.
  4. Seek guidance from industry professionals when exploring paths.
  5. Understand that it's okay to change your mind later.
