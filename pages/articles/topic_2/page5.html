The Dread of the Final Choice

OBILLO, MON ANDREI March 20, 2026 5 min read

I actually dreaded writing this particular blog, because it touches directly into something I actually don’t know the answer to. In my previous blogs, I often explored how I was clueless on what to pursue in my career, and how I often find others being able to find their own path easily. Even now, I don’t know what I’ll commit to. There’s this underlying fear of choosing the wrong path, because I’m afraid that I won’t succeed in my own career because of lack of passion.

Overwhelming Choices

If you do a quick search, you’ll find lots of possible career choices and specializations available for computer science majors. From Software Development, Web Development, Data Science, AI, Cybersecurity, and many others, the list goes on. It’s an overwhelming choice to make, and something that a lot of us, including myself, are afraid to make. Who wouldn’t be afraid of this? You can’t choose all of them, and choosing would mean sticking with it. Choosing a specialization forces you to spend hundreds of hours learning the skills you need to master that specialization. What if, after all of that, you realize you don’t actually want to pursue it? What if you end up not happy with the path you chose?

Loving What You Do

This actually connects well to the previous entry about how you need to love what you do so you don’t run into burn out and ragequitting. The idea of loving what you do is simple enough, but what if you don’t actually know what you love? Trial and Error? Research? The internet says a lot of ways to find out what you want. Being in the same boat, I haven’t been able to answer that question.

Advice from the Tech Industry

I asked some people in the tech industry the same question.

One of them advised me this: explore your options, and don’t force yourself to commit to it immediately. They said that you just have to start, and try it out. There’s a lot of tutorials and samples on the internet, and what matters is you get a glimpse of what you’ll be doing. You don’t even have to understand how it works. If you feel the need to find the answers, and it’s something you think you’ll actually enjoy, then you continue. I asked them, what if you feel this way for all? They said: Then rest. Let your mind wander off for a bit, then come back to it. I thought it was counterintuitive. But upon reflecting, I do think it made sense. Sometimes, we do really have to rest and let ourselves recover, because clarity comes from a clear and well-rested mind. We get so caught up in trying to think about our future that we sometimes forget to reset our minds and rest every once in a while.

Reflection

Looking back, I did fail a lot of times to push through my goals, not only because I burned out, but also because I push myself too hard to fulfill my goal that I forget to rest every once in a while. I realized how much that contributed to that burnout I often get. So, plus one advice on Passion Burnout from the previous blog:

  1. Don’t forget to rest every once in a while.

Moving Forward

I think the best thing we can do for ourselves in this journey of self-exploration is to not pressure ourselves too much. I get it, the looming dread of having to commit to a specific specialization is huge, and I’m afraid as well. And I get it, the last couple of blogs, my reflections so far has been that time’s moving too fast and I have to choose sooner than later. But maybe, just maybe, I don’t have to rush myself too much. I still have time, and so do you. I can tell you a lot about the possible specializations you can take for your future, which I plan for the next blogs in this, and I can recommend you to do what was advised to me: to explore, and to not commit too early, but I’ll also remind you to take a rest once in a while.

Final Thought

We can stop every once in a while, and that’s enough.

Advice

  1. Don’t rush to commit to a specialization; explore first.
  2. Take breaks and rest to gain clarity in decision-making.
  3. Love what you do to prevent burnout.
  4. Remember that your first choice doesn’t have to be your last.
  5. Explore options before fully committing to a path.
  6. Don’t forget to rest every once in a while.
