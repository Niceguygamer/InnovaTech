Suddenly, I’m Halfway There (Wait, Already?)

OBILLO, MON ANDREI March 20, 2026 5 min read

It felt weird realizing it, but somehow I find myself already near halfway through my college life. I barely even noticed how fast everything was going. I still remember my first day in college, and it felt… weird. I didn’t see myself anywhere near that level. People already see me as a “College Student” and an “Adult”, while deep inside I still felt a bit “High School”. Now, at my second, almost third, year in college, I’m still trying to comprehend the idea of it, but do I even have time to accept it before I finish college? I’m already halfway there, and nowhere near what I should be.

A Question That Hit Me

During one of my classes recently, we were talking to our professor about the lesson they were teaching. I wasn’t really into that class at that time, mostly scrolling through my phone and having the professor’s lecture as background noise. To me, it felt like any other day, just lectures and attending classes. I was halfway through a TikTok video when I heard something that was asked by my professor. “Have you guys thought about what specialization you want when you graduate?” That made me stop. The professor was asking us, very casually, and it seemed as though a lot already had something in mind. They were answering the question with ease. But me? I wasn’t sure.

The Thought That Stayed

When classes ended that day, I mostly forgot about the lessons and the topics discussed in the lectures I attended that day. What stuck to me was that burning question, which I guess wasn’t as burning to others because they clearly know what they’re doing. I asked myself the question again: “Have I thought about what specialization I want when I graduate?” Have I? The answer was clear. I haven’t. At this point in my college journey, I still haven’t considered any particular path. I haven’t thought about what job I wanted, or what projects I’d like to pursue, or even what problem I wanted to solve, college thesis-wise. Should I pursue software engineering, or cybersecurity, or web development, or game development, I don’t know.

Looking Back

One of the things I thought about was what exactly pushed me to pursue Computer Science in the first place. Back in my senior high school days, when college applications were being submitted, I tried to apply to various colleges and universities. My actual first choice, and one that, back then, I was so fond of, was Chemistry. Either I took Chemistry or Chemical Engineering. For me, it seemed so cool and interesting to study about the one subject I enjoyed the most when I took the STEM strand in senior high school. My second choice was either Computer Science or Information Technology. When I applied to the university I’m studying in now, I applied for Computer Science. My reason? It seemed cooler to say I’m a computer science major, instead of being the usual IT and there’s no Chemical Engineering here. Funny as it was then, I’m now more than afraid of what I might end up as in the future. The tech industry promised a growing job market and high salary grades, but turns out it’s only for those who truly can push through and find their niche in the field. As for people like me, I have absolutely no idea what to do.

Where I Am Now

Halfway through, and I’m still trying to find my own way. Don’t get me wrong, I love my course, and truly enjoy the subjects I’m taking. But, at a point where almost everyone has a clear direction, I don’t know what I’ll do next.

Advices

  1. Reflect on your goals and whether you’ve thought about your future path.
  2. It’s okay to not have a clear specialization yet; awareness is the first step.
  3. Take time to explore different career paths before committing.
  4. Recognize that being “halfway” through college doesn’t mean you’re behind.
  5. Enjoy learning your subjects and developing skills at your own pace.
