Suddenly, I’m Halfway There (Wait, Already?)

OBILLO, MON ANDREI March 20, 2026 5 min read

It felt weird realizing it, but somehow I find myself already near halfway through my college life. I barely even noticed how fast everything was going. I still remember my first day in college, and it felt weird. People already saw me as a “College Student” and an “Adult”, while deep inside I still felt a bit “High School.” Now, at my second, almost third, year, I’m still trying to comprehend it. But do I even have time to accept it before I finish college?

Halfway, But Not Ready

I’m already halfway there, and nowhere near what I feel like I should be.

During one of my classes recently, we were talking to our professor about the lesson. I wasn’t really into it at that time, mostly scrolling through my phone and treating the lecture as background noise. It felt like any other day.

Then I heard something that made me stop.

“Have you guys thought about what specialization you want when you graduate?”

The question was asked casually, and it seemed like a lot of people already had something in mind. They answered with ease. But me? I wasn’t sure.

The Question That Stayed

When classes ended that day, I forgot most of the lecture. But that question stuck.

I asked myself again: have I thought about what specialization I want?

The answer was simple. I haven’t.

At this point in my college journey, I still haven’t considered a specific path. I don’t know what job I want, what projects I’d like to pursue, or even what problem I want to solve for my thesis. Software engineering, cybersecurity, web development, game development, I don’t know.

Why Did I Even Choose This?

One thing I started questioning was what pushed me to pursue Computer Science in the first place.

Back in senior high school, my first choice was actually Chemistry. Either Chemistry or Chemical Engineering. It felt exciting to study something I genuinely enjoyed in STEM.

Computer Science was only my second option. When I applied to my current university, I chose it simply because it sounded cooler than IT, and Chemical Engineering wasn’t available.

It was a funny reason back then. Now, it feels heavier than it should.

The tech industry promises growth and high salaries, but it’s clear now that it’s really for people who find their niche and push through. As for me, I still have no idea what I’m doing.

Still Figuring It Out

Halfway through, and I’m still trying to find my own way.

Don’t get me wrong, I love my course and genuinely enjoy what I’m learning. But when it feels like everyone else already has direction, it’s hard not to question where I’m headed.

What I Realized

Maybe being halfway doesn’t mean I’m late. Maybe it just means I’m finally aware of where I stand.

And maybe that’s where things actually start.
