Topic # 9

Rest and Depression – Finding Energy Again

March 20, 2026 5 min read


“You don’t have to control your thoughts; you just have to stop letting them control you.” - Dan Millman

Depression often feels like a heavy fog—draining motivation, energy, and joy. Even simple tasks can feel overwhelming. While rest is not a cure for depression, it plays a vital role in recovery. Rest restores energy, stabilizes mood, and creates space for healing.

How Depression Affects Energy

Depression disrupts sleep cycles, reduces motivation, and drains physical and mental energy. This exhaustion makes daily responsibilities harder, creating a cycle of fatigue and hopelessness. Depression not only directly causes fatigue but also indirectly affects sleep, diet, and exercise, draining energy. Depression negatively affects almost every system of the body, increasing the risk for certain physical illnesses or conditions. When experiencing a depressive episode, the brain doesn’t receive the signals that boost moods properly, leading to low energy.

The Role of Rest in Managing Depression

Rest helps break this cycle. Consistent sleep, gentle downtime, and creative breaks support the body and mind. Rest provides the foundation for therapy, medication, and other treatments to work more effectively. Rest is not just about sleep; it includes various activities that can support mental recovery. It helps the body to shift out of a fight-or-flight mode, into a calmer one.

Personal Experience: Rediscovering Energy Through Gentle Pauses

Depression is a serious mental health matter that shouldn’t be dismissed. People who experience this need proper care and someone whom they can trust and rely on. Challenges in life are inevitable, with constant challenges thrown to us, even the strongest ones, tend to fall. During periods of burnout and dark chapters in your life, it’s okay to not be okay. What matters is that we continue to fight little-by-little, day-by-day. Allow yourself to process your emotions once you’ve got proper rest. Rest may not solve everything, but it will give you strength to keep moving forward through gentle pauses with yourself.

Rest as Self-Compassion

Depression often comes with guilt—feeling lazy or unworthy. Rest reframes this narrative. By allowing ourselves to rest, we acknowledge our need for care and begin to build resilience. Rest is a gentle but powerful tool in managing depression. It restores energy, supports healing, and reminds us that recovery is possible.

Top 10 Advice/Tips

  1. Keep a consistent sleep schedule - Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily.
  2. Allow guilt-free downtime - Rest is part of healing, not laziness.
  3. Take short walks or light exercise - Movement boosts mood and energy.
  4. Engage in creative hobbies - Art, music, or writing can spark joy.
  5. Practice relaxation techniques - Breathing, meditation, or yoga.
  6. Limit overstimulation - Reduce screen time before bed.
  7. Seek supportive social rest - Spend time with uplifting people.
  8. Break tasks into small steps - Rest between efforts to avoid overwhelm.
  9. Use naps strategically - Short naps can restore energy without disrupting sleep.
  10. Pair rest with professional support - Therapy and medical care work best when supported by restorative habits.
