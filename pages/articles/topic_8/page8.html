Rest and Anxiety – Calming the Mind

MUNDA, SOFIA ADELAIDE March 20, 2026 5 min read


“Anxiety does not empty tomorrow of its sorrows, but only empties today of its strength.” - Charles Spurgeon

Anxiety thrives on constant stimulation—racing thoughts, endless tasks, and overstimulation from screens and noise. Rest provides the pause that interrupts this cycle. By practicing intentional rest, we calm the nervous system, reduce stress hormones, and create space for clarity.

How Anxiety Affects the Mind

Anxiety keeps the brain in a state of hyper-alertness, flooding the body with cortisol and adrenaline. This makes rest difficult, and without rest, anxiety worsens. It becomes a cycle of exhaustion and worry. Chronic anxiety can disrupt cognitive processes, emotional stability, and overall mental health. These changes increase vulnerability to depression, PTSD, and other mental health disorders.

The Role of Rest in Managing Anxiety

Rest helps regulate the nervous system. Practices like deep breathing, sensory breaks, and mindfulness lower cortisol levels and slow racing thoughts. Rest allows the mind and body to recover and process emotions. Rest acts as a buffer against stress, helping us to recover, recharge emotionally, and regain strength needed for difficult situations we face. Rest allows us to pause, reflect, and develop healthier coping mechanisms.

Personal Experience: Breathing Through Panic

As a certified overthinker, I tend to constantly worry and panic especially when it’s related to school activities and deadlines. My mind always tells me to push harder, but that only makes me panic more. Instead of being able to focus on the things I need to do at school, my mind is exhausting me with constant worrying thoughts. I needed to find a solution for this because I can’t go on like this every day. That’s when I learned to practice mindful rest—closing my eyes, breathing deeply, and pausing for at least five minutes—I noticed I’m starting to be more calm. That pause gave me the strength to continue without worrying too much. That’s when I realized that when these kinds of situations happen, sometimes all we need is a moment of pause—to close our eyes, breathe, and relax our mind.

Rest as a Mental Health Tool

Rest doesn’t erase anxiety, but it equips us to face it. By integrating rest into daily routines, we build resilience and reduce the intensity of anxious episodes. Rest is not a luxury—it is a necessity for managing anxiety. By learning how to pause, we calm our mind, restore balance, and lessen our fears or worries. Life is full of challenges, but by learning how to deal with them in a better way allows us to learn and overcome them without losing our mind.

Top 10 Advice/Tips

1. Practice deep breathing - Inhale for 4 counts, exhale for 6.

2. Take sensory breaks - Step into a quiet, calm environment.

3. Limit caffeine - Reduce triggers that heighten anxiety.

4. Use guided meditation apps - Short sessions can reset the mind.

5. Journal anxious thoughts - Writing them down reduces mental clutter.

6. Stretch or walk - Gentle movement eases tension.

7. Schedule downtime - Treat rest as part of your routine.

8. Disconnect from screens - Reduce overstimulation.

9. Practice gratitude reflection - Shift focus from worry to appreciation.

10. Build a calming ritual - Light a candle, breathe, and pause daily.
