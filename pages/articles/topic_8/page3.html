Emotional Rest—Releasing the Mental Load

MUNDA, SOFIA ADELAIDE March 20, 2026 5 min read

“You don’t have to control your thoughts. You just have to stop letting them control you.” - Dan Millman

Do you carry emotions that never get expressed? Many of us push through daily responsibilities while quietly holding onto frustrations, sadness, or worry. Over time, this emotional load becomes heavy and eventually drains our energy. Emotional rest is about releasing that burden—finding ways to express feelings honestly and lighten the mental weight we carry.

What is Emotional Rest?

Emotional rest means creating space to express feelings without judgement. It’s the relief that comes from honesty—whether through journaling, conversations, or creative outlets. Without it, we risk emotional exhaustion, where even small tasks feel overwhelming. Emotional rest is as important as physical rest. It’s allowing your thoughts to be voiced out outside your mind, that allows you to lessen the weight of carrying them.

The Hidden Cost of Bottled Emotions

Suppressing emotions may seem easier in the moment, but it builds stress over time. Students often hide their frustration to avoid conflict and add things they have to pay attention to. This constant suppression drains mental energy and can lead to burnout. When you constantly dismiss your emotional load, there’s a big tendency that it’ll explode anytime, making you feel overwhelmed with the emotions that are running inside you. It’ll cost you your peace of mind, your positive energy, your mood, and your day-to-day living.

Personal Experience: Journaling Through Stress

Life is already stressful enough. We cannot avoid stressful situations but we can control what we do when they happen. I experienced a lot of stressful things already just by being a student. There goes the pile of school work, deadline after deadline, quizzes, and exams, that all requires me to study and be present. That alone can already be stressful which is why I do journaling to express what I feel. Through journaling, I feel comfortable in letting out my thoughts and what I feel which makes it feel less heavier. I can approach things with more clarity and a clear mind. This helps me to be productive and be more positive in life.

Creating Safe Spaces for Emotional Rest

Emotional rest thrives in a safe environment. Trusted friends, mentors, or therapists can provide spaces where feelings are validated instead of dismissed. Even solitude can be a safe space, where journaling or meditation allows emotions to surface without judgement. Emotional expression improves resilience, strengthens relationships, and enhances overall well-being. Emotional rest didn’t mean ignoring problems; it meant acknowledging them honestly.

Top 10 Advice/Tips

1. Journal daily - Write down worries, frustrations, or gratitude.

2. Share feelings with trusted friends or mentors - Honest conversations reduce emotional weight.

3. Practice mindfulness meditation - Observe emotions without judgement.

4. Allow yourself to cry or laugh - Emotional release is healthy.

5. Avoid toxic positivity - Acknowledge struggles instead of masking them.

6. Seek professional support - Therapists provide safe spaces for deeper emotional rest.

7. Use creative outlets - Art, music, or writing can express emotions indirectly.

8. Set boundaries - Protect yourself from emotionally draining situations.

9. Practice self-compassion - Remind yourself it’s okay to feel.

10. Reflect regularly -> Ask yourself: “What emotions am I carrying today?”
