Leadership Is a Journey, Not a Marathon

ALBARICO, IZYLLE ANNE March 31, 2026 5 min read

Leadership is a journey of growth. Mistakes, reflection, and adaptation are essential for resilience and maturity.

You Are a Leader, but Most of the Time a Learner Learning never stops. Leadership is not about knowing how to lead; it is about becoming better the more you gain experience and handle different people. You will never truly know how to manage every situation because every team is different, and there are always new challenges to encounter. What matters most is your willingness to learn, not the credibility behind your name. There will be times when you feel lost and confused, not knowing what to do. To get through those moments, you must be willing to admit that you don’t know and allow yourself to be a student again. This will give you grace and the ability to make mistakes.

Awareness Leads to Improvement

I have experienced many challenges in my time as a leader since freshman year of college. In every leadership role, I treated myself as a student—applying lessons from past experiences while carrying new lessons from current situations. I never saw myself as above the team. I am a team member as well, but I plan, organize, and turn vision into reality.

I once had a team I considered a “Lesson” because they truly were. I never learned so much from any other team. They helped me realize my weaknesses and strengths, the type of people I want to work with, and what I should look for in a team. I gained awareness because of the challenges I encountered with them, and I am grateful for those opportunities. This team made me realize that challenges and difficulties are not things to avoid—they are signs of shortcomings you need to improve on so you can come out stronger and better.

The source of friction with this team was the difference in work ethic and preferences. Most of the members preferred less structure and more autonomy, while I preferred structure and systems. At first, I thought they were the problem and my system was not. I tried strictly enforcing my system, which only led to resistance. After failed attempts, I realized the system itself might be the problem. When I adapted my system to their preferences instead of forcing mine, everything went smoothly and the contention resolved itself. I wouldn’t have realized this if not for their stubbornness, which I am now grateful for—it gave me knowledge and awareness I wouldn’t have gained otherwise.

Leadership

Leadership is about learning to lead your team better and becoming a reliable guide. It is not about authority or power; it is about being the direction for the team toward success. You are not the face of the team—they are. Let them shine, because people see the results of their effort, talents, skills, and contributions. As a leader, you must enhance that, uphold the highest standards, and give your full support. Help them grow, and together you will reach greater heights.

5 Tips to Overcome the Hurdles of Leadership

  1. Allow yourself to make mistakes and learn.
  2. Take challenges as opportunities to identify your strengths and weaknesses, and improve.
  3. Don’t expect perfection; allow yourself to ask for and receive help.
  4. Believe in your resilience to overcome hardships.
  5. Increase your self-awareness through constant reflection and feedback.
