Let Me Make This Clear: Setting Expectations and Goals

ALBARICO, IZYLLE ANNE March 31, 2026 5 min read

Clear expectations prevent resentment. Transparency in goals and standards ensures fairness and unity within the team.

Clarity and Direction Is Your Greatest Gift to Your Team In a team, it is best to be clear about what you expect from each other in terms of values, triggers, and goals for both the team and the project. All must align so each team member is working toward a single goal and direction. From personal experience, I noticed that when I’m clear with my team about what I value and what triggers me, they behave in ways that prevent them from crossing the boundaries I set. Being clear and honest with your team helps prevent resentment in the long run because they know how you operate and how to work around it.

Honesty, Vulnerability, Compliance

I have a story to share about the difference in how two distinct teams responded to expectations. Team A can be described as lacking unity—a team that only exists to complete a project. Team B can be described as a team with unity and a defined vision or goal. My experiences with both teams were very different. Team A tended not to acknowledge expectations and continued to act as before, while Team B, though they might lack acknowledgement, aligned their actions with the expectations. Did Team A eventually comply? The answer is yes, but not because expectations were set directly. Instead, compliance came through creating a system that guided them to meet expectations. I noticed that Team A was more transactional than relational compared to Team B, which simply required sharing expectations for them to follow through. With Team B, I felt more like part of a team than just coworkers, because we were working together toward a single goal.

Sharing expectations is vulnerable, but it is better to make them known than to hold resentment toward others for not meeting them when they don’t even know what they are. To ensure compliance, you should adapt your strategy based on the type of team you are handling or managing, because they all operate differently. You cannot apply the same system to all.

Expectations

Expectations are about sharing your “user manual” with your team—how you operate, what you expect from them, and what you want to achieve together. This helps the team work smoothly, prevents resentment, and promotes communication. People will want to act in ways that prevent known triggers from being activated.

5 Tips to Set Expectations & Goals

  1. Create a list of what you value, your triggers, and your goals for both the team and the project.
  2. Be honest with yourself and with them, as this will serve as the basis for behaviors.
  3. Adjust your delivery based on the team you are handling.
  4. Enforce those expectations and make the goals happen.
  5. Practice empathy and understanding since humans are flawed and shortcomings are unavoidable, no matter how many times expectations are set—we are just human.
