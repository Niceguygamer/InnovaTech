Tell Me Your Ideas, I’ll Make It Happen: Promoting Collaboration & Brainstorming

ALBARICO, IZYLLE ANNE March 31, 2026 5 min read

Innovation thrives on multiple voices. Collaboration transforms individual ideas into meaningful, collective solutions.

You guys run the show, and I'm just the conductor orchestrating it. I'm part of the team, not above it. This is what I told my team when affirming to them that I don’t just give orders—I experience the same hardship and pressure they face. If they have one job, then I have a job that accounts for the whole entire time. Collaboration is significant because it brings innovation to a simple project, sets the direction and vision, and provides the purpose and motivation to continue working. At one point, I even considered collaboration my favorite process of team work because you really combine ideas and make them come to life.

What Makes a Project Excellent

What makes a project excellent is not one idea, but multiple ideas. I have handled multiple projects where every output we produced was something we were proud of and found meaningful. The top three projects that stood out to me were:

  1. School Play – The theme was Western and Eastern philosophy. The script was written by three writers and organized and planned by me. It was successful because we executed everything according to plan and with quality. I was proud of it, and after the event, the team celebrated together.
  2. Group Project Presentation – We had to present our work creatively. My team chose a game show theme where all of the boys, including me, wore dresses and danced, while one member played both the audience and host. This was successful too, earning us first place for the presentation.
  3. Website Project – We shared ideas on what topic our website should be about. Out of 10 submissions, we settled on “The Complete Survival Guide to Thriving in Tech-Related College Programs.” This was meaningful because it resonated with all of us.

Understand the Idea, Don’t Filter It

From all my team collaboration experiences, I learned it is best to simply understand suggestions instead of filtering them immediately. Guide ideas toward feasibility or make them feasible. If an idea is too ambitious for the resources available or unsuitable for the limited timeframe, then reject it—but still acknowledge that it was valued and considered. It’s all about making team members feel they are part of something meaningful. When each person in the team feels their contribution mattered, they feel responsible for their task and role.

Collaboration

Collaboration is about working together to create something you all believe in. Combining ideas from multiple perspectives leads to projects that are innovative, creative, and meaningful. When people feel involved, they find meaning in what they do. This helps them feel motivated and driven to power through challenges. Being in a team is about consensus and unity, not individuality.

5 Tips to Promote Collaboration and Brainstorming

  1. Set up discussions where each team member shares their ideas.
  2. Listen, understand, and be open to suggestions.
  3. Guide ideas toward feasibility—don’t outright reject them.
  4. Involve team members in the decision-making process and ensure everyone is clear on the project’s purpose and vision.
  5. If an idea is truly not possible due to limited resources, acknowledge it and reject it politely.
