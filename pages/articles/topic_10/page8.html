That’s Not Okay: Uphold Boundaries, Don’t Tolerate Disrespect

ALBARICO, IZYLLE ANNE March 31, 2026 5 min read

Boundaries protect professionalism and dignity. Respect is non-negotiable, and leaders must enforce accountability firmly.

Don’t be afraid to uphold boundaries—they are the blueprint for how people should treat you. There are situations where emotions such as anger, frustration, and stress can take the upper hand. Conflicts between members can also become personal. Remember, don’t assume people know how to treat you unless you clearly set your boundaries. If they react badly or leave, that reveals more about them than about you. Boundaries help filter people who truly respect and value your time. It’s less about the number of people around you and more about the quality of company you keep.

Don’t Negotiate Boundaries

I have had several experiences where deadlines were always the problem in groups. There were delays and certain members who didn’t uphold accountability for their tasks. They often made excuses or only moved when prompted. One team member I call “Jokester” was great as a friend but difficult as a collaborator. I had to adjust myself and have many private talks with him about deadlines being hard cutoffs, not suggestions.

Because of repeated letdowns and lack of follow-through, I developed strictness and firmness with him, which subconsciously extended to the rest of the team. I spoke to him through chat, explaining that this behavior was unacceptable regardless of life situations—we must follow through on commitments. That’s how I established boundaries. I don’t let things slide, because if I did, the behavior would continue. “Jokester” apologized and reflected on his actions, and recently he has been trying to comply on time. I’ll wait to see if this consistency holds, because that matters more than one-time compliance. As leaders, we should give members room to change while ensuring team progress and performance are not sacrificed.

Setting Clear Boundaries

Boundaries allow us to work together in a healthy and respectful way where no one feels violated or dismissed. They make the team feel valued because everyone knows what not to do and what they can do. This is vital for fostering professionalism, especially since moods and emotions can be influenced by heated moments. I once had a team member curse at me out of frustration or say insensitive words directly to my face. If that isn’t disrespect, I don’t know what is. Because of this, one of the policies we created to repair our working environment was “No Tolerance for Unprofessionalism.” It stated that curses, accusatory remarks, and insensitive words are unacceptable and not tolerated. If such behavior escalates or repeats, intervention will be made. Boundaries are the key to creating a respectful and safe environment for everyone.

5 Steps to Set Clear Boundaries

  1. Share a list of actions you won’t tolerate if committed.
  2. Hold a meeting to discuss the list and encourage members to share theirs as well.
  3. Create policies based on the list and reach consensus on consequences if not met.
  4. Implement the policies and follow through with consequences.
  5. Regularly adjust the policies based on what worked and what didn’t.
