Topic # 5

Promoting Positive Relationships

LIBUTON, CYRIL MAE March 20, 2026 5 min read

Creating Meaningful Connections

College relationships are not all the same. While some individuals become a part of your support network, others could not have a favorable impact on your development. It's critical to learn how to distinguish the differences.

Open communication, respect, and trust are the cornerstones of healthy relationships. These connections develop as a result of common experiences, understanding, and unwavering support. These relationships can be strengthened by even seemingly insignificant gestures, such as offering assistance or checking in on a classmate.

Setting boundaries is crucial. It's critical to maintain your wellbeing, time, and energy. Not all relationships should be sustained, particularly if they are detrimental to you.

Developing solid relationships adds significance to your time in college. The people you interact with can help you overcome obstacles, rejoice in your accomplishments, and add special moments to your life.

Practical Tips for Interpersonal Relationships

  • Communicate Clearly and Respectfully

    Express your thoughts honestly while considering others' feelings.

  • Practice Active Listening

    Pay full attention, ask questions, and confirm understanding.

  • Respect Different Perspectives

    Recognize that others may see things differently and value their views.

  • Take Responsibility in Group Work

    Contribute your share and follow through on commitments.

  • Address Conflicts Calmly

    Stay composed and focus on resolving issues, not blaming.

  • Show Appreciation for Others

    Recognize efforts and express gratitude to strengthen relationships.

  • Set Healthy Boundaries

    Know your limits and communicate them clearly to maintain balance.

  • Study Together with Friends

    Collaborate on learning to help each other understand and succeed.

  • Be Supportive Towards Each Other

    Encourage and help friends through challenges and achievements.
