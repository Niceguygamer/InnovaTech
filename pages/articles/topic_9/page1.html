Topic # 9

You Are Not Lazy, You Are Running on Empty

LEONGSUN, EUWIN March 31, 2026 5 min read

There is a specific kind of tired that sleep does not fix. You wake up already exhausted. You sit down to work and nothing comes out. You used to care about this stuff and now you just cannot make yourself. That is not laziness. That is burnout.

I know what burnout feels like because I have been in it. Not the mild "I need a break" version. The kind where you open your project and just stare at it for twenty minutes without touching anything. The kind where you used to get excited about building something and now the thought of it just makes you tired.

A lot of students in tech programs hit this at some point and blame themselves for it. They think they are not working hard enough, or they are not cut out for this, or everyone else is handling it fine and there is something wrong with them. Usually none of that is true.

Burnout Is Not the Same as Being Tired

Being tired means you need rest. You sleep, you recover, you come back. Burnout is different. It builds up over a long time from sustained pressure, and it does not go away after one good night of sleep or a weekend off. It is your mind and body telling you that something about the way you have been operating is not sustainable.

The signs are not always obvious at first. You start taking longer to do things that used to come easily. You lose interest in stuff you actually liked doing. Small problems feel enormous. You get irritable over nothing. Your output drops even when you are putting in the same amount of time, sometimes more.

In a tech program, these signs are easy to dismiss because the workload is always heavy. You tell yourself you are just having a rough week. Then it becomes a rough month. By the time you realize what is actually happening, you are already deep in it.

Why It Matters to Name It Correctly

If you think you are lazy, you push harder. That makes burnout worse. If you think you are just tired, you try to sleep it off and nothing changes. But if you recognize that you are burnt out, you can actually do something useful about it.

The rest of this series is about understanding where burnout comes from in tech programs specifically, what it looks like when it is affecting your work, and what you can actually do to recover and build something more sustainable. Not to avoid hard work. Just to make sure the hard work does not end up breaking you.
