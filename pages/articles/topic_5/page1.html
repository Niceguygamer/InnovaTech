Topic # 1

You Signed Up For This
(But Nobody Warned You)

AGUNOD, IÑIGO JOSE March 20, 2026 5 min read

Nobody warns you about the 3 am silence - when your code still won't compile, your energy drink is cold, and your roommate is peacefully asleep. If you are in a tech-related program, you already know that feeling personally. This is the reality check every tech student evetually faces, and the sooner you understand it, the better off you will be.

There is an unspoken culture in IT, computer science, and software engineering courses that quietly rewards suffering. Who stayed up latest. Who has the most tabs open. Who skipped lunch to finish a function. Somewhere along the way, exhaustion became a personality trait and grinding became the only accepted strategy. You stop asking whether your approach is actually working and start measuring yourself purely by how destroyed you feel at the end of the day.

A Wall Nobody Talks About

Most tech students hit a breaking point around second semester. Suddenly you are expected to learn a new programming language, finish a group project, pass a theory exam, and keep your grades up across five subjects — all at the same time. When you fall behind, the first instinct is to blame yourself. To think you are just not cut out for this field. But the more honest truth is that the system was never designed around your actual cognitive limits. The students who make it are not the ones who suffer the most — they are the ones who figured out early that studying smarter is a skill you have to deliberately build.

The overwork mentality is not a badge of honor. It is a trap. And the first step to getting out of it is simply recognizing that it exists — and deciding you are allowed to approach this differently.

Tips to Remember

  • Track one full week of your time honestly — most of it is probably panic, not actual studying.
  • Separate "looking busy" from "actually learning" — your grades will eventually show the difference.
  • Talk to an upperclassman about how they manage the load most had to unlearn overwork too.
  • Drop one draining low-value task from your week — you will not miss it.
  • Struggling with hard technical material is normal — it does not mean you do not belong here.
