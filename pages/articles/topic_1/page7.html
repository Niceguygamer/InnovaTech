School Loads and Class Standing

ESICO, BRYAN March 20, 2026 5 min read

The Systemic Perspective Individual effort can only go so far if the system itself is flawed. For a long time, the burden of managing academic stress has been placed entirely on the student. But as the mental health crisis escalates, it is clear that institutions must change. We need a structural rebuild that prioritizes quality of instruction over the sheer volume of assessment.

Assessment Modality The "continuous assessment" model was supposed to help students stay on track, but in many cases, it just created perpetual pressure. Students are calling for curricula that focus on practical, meaningful knowledge. Institutional policies that allow for more flexibility in deadlines and workload distribution could significantly improve student well being without sacrificing academic rigor.

The Structural Rebuild

The Role of Faculty Professors and instructors are the ones who design the workload. Training faculty in cognitive load management and trauma informed pedagogy could change the student experience from the top down. When faculty members communicate expectations clearly and coordinate deadlines across departments, the entire academic environment becomes more sustainable.

Conclusion We need institutions that care as much about student well being as they do about academic rankings. Structural change is the only long term solution.

List of Advice

  • Provide feedback to your institution about workload issues.
  • Look for classes that use diverse assessment methods.
  • Utilize office hours to clarify ambiguous assignments.
  • Support student organizations that advocate for mental health.
  • Choose electives that offer a different pace of learning.
