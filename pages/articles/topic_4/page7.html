Managing Your Time with Scheduling and Calendar Apps

ALAB-AB, CHARLIZE March 20, 2026 5 min read

"Time is what we want most, but what we use worst." Early in college, I always felt like I had enough time, until suddenly I began to notice how quickly time passed. Deadlines were creeping up on me without warning, all of my review materials were not prepared ahead of time and all of my major assignments were due tomorrow. By using scheduling applications to implement a structure to my day-to-day activities has made all the difference.

"I'll do it later" and "there's still time" led to a lot of added stress for me, particularly when I under-estimated how long it actually takes me to complete things, coding assignments that require extensive attention to detail. Then everything changed when I began to schedule my week out ahead of time, creating one clear schedule with classes, deadlines, review sessions, and group projects. This helped me feel like I had control of what I was doing, as well as helping me stay one step ahead of being behind.

Stop Reacting to Deadlines, Start Planning Ahead

At the beginning of every week, I write down the items I need to do and then allocate time blocks within my schedule to study, practice and work on the various projects that I will be working on. What resulted from that was that I was able to use my time more intentionally and productively than ever before. I also discovered that a perfect schedule is not one that is going to be jam-packed all the time but instead, it is necessary to include some sort of buffer time in between activities such as lounging, relaxing, and resting, in order for me to be able to realistically sustain this process. And, if any of those activities didn't go as originally planned, being willing to adapt the scheduling of future activities will help me remain consistent and not return to procrastination.

Calendar and scheduling applications have helped me in effectively managing my Daily Routines, rather than allowing the time of day to dictate my actions. At the same time, these applications provided me with some level of order, understanding, and balance while I was attending college; thus, I was able to maintain organization and preparedness, rather than feeling that I would always be behind.

Tips to Remember

  • Plan your week ahead – Stay organized and prepared.
  • Set reminders – Never miss important deadlines.
  • Include breaks – Avoid burnout.
  • Prioritize important tasks – Focus on what matters most.
  • Review your schedule daily – Stay on track.
