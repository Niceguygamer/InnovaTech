Expanding Knowledge with Learning and Resources Apps

ALAB-AB, CHARLIZE March 20, 2026 5 min read

Many of the topics I was exposed to in my tech program did not make sense through just the lecture. The instructor would move on after every slide, and I would be right there, nodding along, but not understanding a word. I often hoped that when I reviewed my notes later, I would have an "Aha!" moment; there weren't many of those. With the introduction of learning and resource apps, I now had a second entry point to the difficult subjects. App creators provided me with additional explanations, in many different formats, as well as allowing me to revisit the hard-to-understand concepts on my schedule.

Learning Doesn't Stop in the Classroom

I found that relying only on lectures to learn about your course, specifically through short lectures and long explanations did not give me enough of an understanding of the subject matter when studying. The use of learning applications helped fill this gap by allowing me to review video tutorials, step-by-step guidelines, practice problems, and real-life examples anytime I need to. The ability to pause, rewind, or adjust the speed/language in which I was learning gave me a greater degree of control over how quickly I learned, particularly with regard to difficult topics. Many times what I thought was confusing in the classroom was easily understood if presented differently. This makes a difference in technology since there may be a solution to solving a problem based upon the information you have regarding any problem or situation.

Additionally to assisting with learning through video tutorials, these applications provided me with the opportunity to practice my skills with quizzes and coding challenges by employing those skills right away, which helped me get better at them faster. These applications were also useful for keeping me up to date on new technology information with the latest content from various types of courses. However, I also learned not to be overwhelmed by the amount of information I had available to me and to only use a few reliable sources to supplement my lecture experience for reinforcement reasons and not to replace my coursework.

Tips to Remember

  • Use apps as support tools – Not replacements for classes.
  • Learn at your own pace – Don't rush.
  • Replay difficult lessons – Strengthen understanding.
  • Avoid jumping between apps – Stay consistent.
  • Build a habit of learning – Keep improving daily.
