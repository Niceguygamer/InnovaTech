Sharpening Skills with Coding Practice Applications

March 20, 2026 5 min read

In my past experience of understanding lectures/speeches and reviewing my notes, I still found difficulty if I attempted to actually write computer programming code. I quickly learned (the hard way) that the typical tech related course format of just learning about a subject wasn’t enough by itself, you must also apply it. It was not until I became aware of coding-related practice applications that I made this type of learning much more effective and interactive.

Initially, my reliance was on videos and notes, I assumed I would be able to employ these concepts immediately to accomplish something after I understood what they were. However, when presented with real-world coding problems, I would often find myself lost. The main thing that was missing was practice.

Learn by Doing, Not Just Reading

By using coding practice tools, I was able to learn new concepts through the application of the knowledge learned from exercises, challenges, and real-world applications of concepts I had learned before. Rather than passively reviewing coding syntax or logic, I was actively writing code to demonstrate my learning and how I made mistakes, so that I could learn from them. In addition, these coding practice platforms exposed me to different types of coding problems, which was advantageous in developing an analytical thought process and in improving my problem-solving abilities. As a result of this continual exposure and practice, I also manifested an improvement in my ability to employ and find solutions to my problems.

Progress in technology programs does not occur through the mere possession of knowledge, it occurs through the application of that knowledge.

Tips to Remember

  • Practice daily – Even 15–30 minutes helps build consistency.
  • Start simple – Don’t jump into advanced problems too quickly.
  • Focus on understanding solutions – Not just getting the answer.
  • Don’t fear mistakes – They are part of learning.
  • Apply what you learn – Connect practice with your schoolwork.
