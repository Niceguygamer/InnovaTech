Mastering Note-Taking Apps

ALAB-AB, CHARLIZE March 20, 2026 5 min read

Have you ever filled pages of notes during a lecture, only to stare at them blankly when exams hit? In tech-related programs where concepts like algorithms and coding logic constantly build on each other, surface-level note-taking isn't just ineffective, it's a trap. That realization led me to discover note-taking apps, and they completely transformed not just how I organized information, but how I actually understood it.

At the start of my college, I took handwritten notes and had spare sheets of paper to document important events in my life. These methods were fine for my minor courses, but quickly became useless when it came time to study programming or other technical subjects. Diagrams are often too small when drawn on regular sized paper and are difficult to change once they are finished. Therefore, I turned to digital note taking applications, which offer me the flexibility to organize my notes by subject, provide separate sections for key ideas, and code examples. As a result, I began to understand the material that I was studying more completely than I would have done had I only been copying notes.

Build Notes That Actually Help You Learn

In order to improve my note-taking, I began utilizing some applications, such as: Goodnotes allowed me to create notes in handwriting or draw diagrams, Notion lets me create structured digital notes and store code snippets, and Evernote helps capture ideas or highlights during lectures quickly. All these applications allowed me to consolidate everything I needed into one location, and thus make my notes more effective when studying. My notes turned into a place where I could create connections between various subjects of study or concepts and be able to reference what I need by performing a simple search, a feature that really aided me during exams.

If you want to succeed in a tech-related program, don’t just take notes, build notes that actually help you learn.

Tips to Remember

  • Organize by subject and topic – Keep everything structured.
  • Use headings and sections – Make notes easy to scan.
  • Include examples – Especially for coding concepts.
  • Don't over-write – Focus on key ideas only..
  • Use highlights and formatting – Emphasize important points.
