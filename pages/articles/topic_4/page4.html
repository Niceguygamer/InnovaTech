Boosting Memory with Flashcard and Study Apps

March 20, 2026 5 min read

In the past, I thought that studying was simply about repeatedly going through my notes until something stuck. Although this took a lot of time and effort, when I took a test, especially when the subject matter was primarily programming concepts, I would be completely blank when tested on very simple concepts. The problem was not the quantity of my studies, it was how I was studying. Learning about using flash cards and memory apps changed my entire study approach.

Study Smarter, Remember More

Although I felt like I had accomplished something while reviewing and highlighting my notes, doing this still did not help me develop the ability to independently recall material, the skills needed to pass an exam. Changing to an active recall process allowed me to focus on testing myself by asking questions, instead of only passively reviewing my materials which made studying harder, but much more productive than passive review methods. The use of digital flashcards was helpful in supporting this, as I was required to recall all of the key concepts, definitions, syntax, processes, etc., from memory. I learned to place a higher emphasis on the quality of the material being reviewed rather than on the amount of material I was reviewing. Along with practice problems and organized notes to help with deeper comprehension, this created a more equitable and productive approach to studying.

Using flashcards and memory applications changed the way I remembered everything I learned while studying for my major courses. If you've been having trouble remembering the things you've learned, then maybe it's time to look at how you're studying. Sometimes, it doesn't require more studying, but rather requires different studying techniques.

Tips to Remember

  • Use active recall – Test yourself, don't just reread.
  • Keep cards simple – One idea per card.
  • Focus on key concepts – Avoid unnecessary details.
  • Study daily – Even short sessions help.
  • Review difficult cards more often – Target weaknesses.
