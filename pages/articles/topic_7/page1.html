Understanding Self-Identity

LIBUTON, CYRIL MAE March 20, 2026 5 min read

Knowing Who You Are

College life can feel overwhelming, especially when everything seems fast-paced and demanding. With deadlines, expectations, and constant comparisons, it is easy to lose focus on yourself.

“Knowing yourself is the beginning of all wisdom.” — Aristotle

Understanding who you are outside of your accomplishments or grades is the essence of self-identity. It encompasses your beliefs, values, hobbies, and self-perception. Self-identity development is a crucial component of personal development.

Sometimes you can compare your progress to others' or feel uncertain about where you're headed. These emotions are typical. How you react to them is what counts. Clarifying your ideas and feelings can be achieved by journaling, meditating, or having conversations about these situations.

Making better decisions is aided by self-awareness. You start making decisions that are consistent with your own objectives and principles rather than just behaving what others do. Additionally, self-awareness enables you to better manage stress and difficulties by identifying your emotional triggers and reactions.

Ways to Stay True to Yourself

  • Check In with Your "Why"

    It’s easy to do things just because everyone else is doing them. Take a second to ask yourself if your goals are actually yours, or if you’re just following the crowd. When your actions line up with your actual values, everything feels a lot less like a chore.

  • Stop the Comparing

    Social media and grades make it feel like you’re always behind, but everyone is on a different timeline. Focus on your own progress—even the tiny wins—instead of measuring your life against someone else’s highlight reel.

  • Find Your Zen

    Whether it’s journaling, a quick walk, or just sitting in silence, you need space to hear your own thoughts. Getting away from the noise of deadlines helps you process how you’re actually feeling before things get overwhelming.

  • Be Easy On Yourself

    You’re going to mess up, and that’s fine. Instead of beating yourself up over a bad grade or a mistake, treat yourself with the same patience you’d give a best friend. Self-identity is about growth, and growth is messy.

  • Trust Your Gut

    Your intuition is usually a pretty good compass. If a project, a group, or a path feels "off," pay attention to that. Learning to trust your own judgment is one of the most important skills you’ll pick up in college.

  • Value Your Passions

    Don't lose the things that make you *you* just because you're busy. Whether it's music, gaming, or a weird hobby, keep time for the stuff that makes you happy. Those are the pieces of your identity that will last long after graduation.
