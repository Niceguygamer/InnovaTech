Topic # 3

Building Self-Confidence

LIBUTON, CYRIL MAE March 20, 2026 5 min read

Growing Through Experience

Confidence is not something you are born with—it is something you build over time. It develops through experiences, challenges, and small achievements. Each step you take, no matter how small, contributes to your growth and shapes how you view yourself.

“You gain strength, courage, and confidence by every experience in which you really stop to look fear in the face.” — Eleanor Roosevelt

You will occasionally have self-doubt, particularly when comparing your advancement to that of others. Being superior to others is not the source of true confidence; rather, it stems from having faith in your capacity to develop. Everybody moves at their own speed, and each person's definition of development is unique. Honor your own path and embrace your adventure.

Trying new things, participating in activities, and stepping out of your comfort zone all contribute to building confidence. Even small successes, like completing a task or speaking up in class, can make a difference. These moments may seem minor, but they gradually build a stronger sense of self-belief.

Realizing that failure is a necessary part of the process is crucial. Every encounter will not go according to plan, and that is rather common. Failure can be seen as a chance to grow and learn rather than as a setback. Every error teaches you important lessons that eventually make you more resilient and self-assured.

Over time, these experiences shape how you see yourself. The more you challenge yourself, the more confident you become. With patience and consistency, confidence grows naturally and becomes a strong foundation for facing future challenges.

Tips for Building Confidence

  • Maintain Your Individual Progress

    It’s so easy to look around the classroom and feel like everyone else has it completely figured out. Remember that true confidence isn’t about being better than anyone else; it’s about trusting your own ability to grow. Focus on your own pace.

  • Recognize Achievements

    Confidence doesn't just magically appear overnight. It’s built on tiny victories—like finally raising your hand during a lecture, or just showing up to a study group when you were feeling anxious. Give yourself credit for the small stuff; it really does add up.

  • Embrace Constructive Challenges

    You don't have to completely overhaul your personality, but try stepping out of your comfort zone just a little bit. Try a new club, or strike up a conversation with the person sitting next to you. Getting comfortable with feeling a little awkward is how you actually grow.

  • Learn from Setbacks

    You are going to mess up. A project will flop, or things won't go according to plan. That’s literally just part of the college experience. Instead of letting a failure ruin your week, look at what went wrong, adjust your strategy, and move on. Every mistake just makes you tougher.

  • Allow Time for Development

    You can't speed-run personal growth. Building a solid foundation of self-belief takes patience and consistency. Just keep showing up, keep pushing yourself slightly, and that quiet confidence will start to feel totally natural.
