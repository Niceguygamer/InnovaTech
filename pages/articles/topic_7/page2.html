Discovering Strengths, Overcoming Challenges

LIBUTON, CYRIL MAE March 20, 2026 5 min read

Everyone has strengths and weaknesses, and understanding both is essential for growth. Your strengths are the skills and qualities that help you succeed, while your weaknesses are areas where you can improve.

Accepting Yourself Fully

It is simple to concentrate on one's shortcomings, particularly in a setting as competitive as college. But dwelling on your shortcomings all the time can undermine your self-assurance. It's critical to acknowledge your special talents and appreciate little wins.

In my experience, I used to concentrate more on my shortcomings than on my strengths. As time went on, I came to understand that while addressing my shortcomings helped me progressively get better, recognizing my strengths helped me gain confidence. Finding your primary skills might help you select the activities, classes, and even career choices that work best for you.

It takes time for growth to occur. It requires dedication, hard work, and persistence. You can develop in a balanced and healthful way when you fully accept who you are. Recall that flaws are chances to grow and learn rather than failures.

Real-World Tips for Playing to Your Strengths

  • Own Your Wins:

    Stop brushing off compliments! If you aced a presentation or wrote a killer paper, let yourself be proud of it. Acknowledging what you're good at builds the confidence you need to tackle the hard stuff later.

  • Flip the Script on Flaws:

    Instead of saying "I suck at math," try "Math takes me a little longer to get." It sounds cheesy, but changing how you talk about your struggles actually makes them feel a lot more manageable.

  • Tag Team Your Weaknesses:

    You don't have to be perfect at everything. If you're a great writer but terrible at designing slides, find a groupmate who is the exact opposite.

  • Lean Into Your Natural Vibe:

    If you're an introvert, don't force yourself to be the loudest person in the room just to stand out. Your ability to listen and think deeply is a massive strength. Play the game your way.

  • Give Yourself Grace:

    Growth is exhausting. You are going to have days where you feel completely incompetent, and that’s completely normal. Take a breath, step away from the laptop, and try again tomorrow.
